Understanding Blood Sugar Levels

Blood sugar levels are typically measured in two units:

  • mg/dL: Milligrams per deciliter, commonly used in the United States and some European countries.
  • mmol/L: Millimoles per liter, standard in the UK, Canada, Australia, and many other countries.

Conversion formulas:

  • mg/dL to mmol/L: Divide by 18
  • mmol/L to mg/dL: Multiply by 18

Normal blood sugar ranges:

  • Fasting (non-diabetic): 70–100 mg/dL (3.9–5.5 mmol/L)
  • Fasting (diabetic target): 70–130 mg/dL (3.9–7.2 mmol/L)
  • Post-meal (2 hours): Less than 180 mg/dL (10 mmol/L)

Maintaining blood sugar within these ranges is crucial for overall health. Consult with healthcare professionals for personalized advice.
